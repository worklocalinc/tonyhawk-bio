Tony Hawk

The Birdman • Skateboarding Legend • Entrepreneur

The Icon Who Transformed Skateboarding

Anthony Frank Hawk, born May 12, 1968, is an American professional skateboarder, entrepreneur, and the most influential skateboarder of all time. Known for completing the first documented "900" skateboarding trick, Hawk has been instrumental in transforming skateboarding from a fringe activity to a respected sport.

Early Life

Born in San Diego, California, Tony Hawk was a hyperactive child who found his calling on a skateboard at age 9. His older brother Steve gave him a blue fiberglass banana board, changing his life forever. By 14, he was already a professional skateboarder, and by 16, he was widely considered the best vertical skateboarder in the world.

Career Highlights

X Games Dominance

12 X Games gold medals and numerous championship titles throughout the 1990s and 2000s.

The 900

On June 27, 1999, at the X Games, Hawk landed the first-ever 900 (two and a half aerial spins) after 11 failed attempts, cementing his legacy.

Video Game Empire

The "Tony Hawk's Pro Skater" video game series, launched in 1999, became one of the most successful sports game franchises ever.

Beyond Skateboarding

Hawk is not just an athlete but a successful entrepreneur. He founded Birdhouse Skateboards, Hawk Clothing, and the Tony Hawk Foundation (now The Skatepark Project), which helps build skateparks in underserved communities. His business ventures and endorsements have made him one of the wealthiest action sports athletes.

Personal Life

Tony has been married four times and has four children. His son Riley is also a professional skateboarder. Despite numerous injuries throughout his career, including a fractured skull, Hawk continues to skate well into his 50s, inspiring new generations of skateboarders.

Legacy

Tony Hawk's impact on skateboarding is immeasurable. He transformed the sport from counterculture rebellion to mainstream acceptance, inspired millions to pick up a skateboard, and continues to be an ambassador for the sport. His humility, business acumen, and dedication to giving back have made him not just a sports icon, but a cultural phenomenon.

"I consider skateboarding an art form, a lifestyle and a sport. 'Action sport' would be the least offensive categorization." - Tony Hawk
